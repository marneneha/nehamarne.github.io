About Me

A Roboticist, a Hodophile, and an Eclectic

I am a first-year graduate student in Robotics at the University of Maryland, College Park. My interests lie in the fields of Multi-agent systems, Machine Learning, and Reinforcement Learning. I have a strong background in developing autonomous systems, as demonstrated by my previous projects. I worked with AEPL after my graduation to lead a team to build a swarm of drones for flocking and foraging, where we developed the UAVs from scratch. Since the dealine for the task was 2 monts we developed on an existing repository. The main challange here was that the repository was written in a new research-based language called "BUZZ". Despite the challenges, we were successful in our efforts and even had the opportunity to demonstrate our work for the Indian army.

I also worked with Dr. Martin Saska at the Multi-Robot System Lab in Prague, where I focused on coherent swarming with minimum computation. During my undergraduate studies, I was part of a team that developed a Library for the development of UGV swarms and applied this to a "search and rescue operation" competition at IIT Madras. I have experience with basic machine learning algorithms such as Bayesian classifiers, SVM, and K-Means clustering. My goal is to develop robots that can adapt to their environment. When I am not questioning my life choices, I listen to music or travel.
